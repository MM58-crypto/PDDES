How the score is calculated?


In case you're interested in knowing how the score is calculated:

each response option is assigned a score of 0, 1, 2, or 3. The score assigned to each option typically reflects the level of distress or impairment reported by the respondent. The specific scoring values may vary slightly depending on the scoring method used, but the most commonly used values are as follows:


0 - Better than usual 1 - No more than usual 2 - Rather more than usual 3 - Much more than usual For example, for question 1 ("Have you recently felt constantly under strain?"), if the respondent answers "Better than usual," they would be assigned a score of 0. If they answer "No more than usual," they would be assigned a score of 1. If they answer "Rather more than usual," they would be assigned a score of 2. And if they answer "Much more than usual," they would be assigned a score of 3.


The other 11 questions would be scored similarly, with each response receiving a score of 0, 1, 2, or 3. A total score is generated when all 12 items have been scored, and it can vary from 0 to 36. Higher scores indicate greater levels of psychological distress or disability.
